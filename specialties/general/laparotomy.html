Laparotomy

A laparotomy is a surgical procedure involving a large incision through the abdominal wall to gain access into the abdominal cavity. It can be exploratory or for specific pathology such as bowel obstruction, perforation, bleeding, or mass excision. This guide outlines the general approach to a laparotomy for bowel resection.

Relevant Anatomy

The laparotomy explores abdominal contents, often involving the stomach, small intestine, colon, appendix, omentum, mesentery, peritoneum, and major vessels. The surgeon may operate on the small bowel (jejunum or ileum), large bowel (e.g. sigmoid colon), appendix, or remove adhesions.

1. Instruments and Equipment Checklist

Basic Laparotomy Tray (Sterile)

  • Scalpel blades (No. 10, 11, 15)
  • Metzenbaum and Mayo scissors
  • Tissue and dissecting forceps
  • Needle holders
  • Small and large retractors (Richardson, Deaver, Balfour)
  • Suction tips (Yankauer and Poole)
  • Debakey forceps, bowel clamps (Doyen), and haemostats
  • Electrocautery (bovie)

Sutures

  • Vicryl 2-0, 3-0
  • PDS or Monocryl for deep layers
  • Nylon for skin or staples

Other Equipment

  • Suction setup with sterile tubing
  • Sterile bowls for irrigation (saline)
  • Gauze, laparotomy pads
  • Specimen containers
  • Foley catheter and nasogastric tube (if required)

2. Before Knife to Skin

  1. Check and prepare all instruments and equipment
  2. Complete initial count of instruments and gauze
  3. Patient identity, consent, and surgical site verification
  4. Assist with prepping and draping the abdomen

Patient Positioning

  • Position: Supine
  • Arms: Tucked or out on armboards
  • Padding: All pressure points well protected

Skin Prepping and Draping

  • Prep entire abdomen with alcoholic chlorhexidine or iodine
  • Allow full drying before sterile draping
  • Apply large abdominal drape

3. Intraoperative Stages

  1. Midline Incision: Skin, subcutaneous tissue, fascia and peritoneum opened using scalpel and scissors
  2. Explore Abdominal Cavity: Suction excess fluid; inspect bowel, stomach, liver, spleen
  3. Identify Pathology: Localise obstruction, perforation, ischemic or necrotic bowel
  4. Bowel Resection: Apply Doyen clamps, resect diseased segment, pass specimen to nurse
  5. Anastomosis or Stoma Formation: End-to-end bowel join or stoma formed with sutures/stapler
  6. Check Haemostasis: Inspect mesentery and bowel for bleeding, apply cautery or clips
  7. Irrigation: Irrigate peritoneal cavity with warm saline and suction clear
  8. Close Abdomen: Layered closure: peritoneum, fascia, subcutaneous, then skin/staples

4. Post-Op Tasks

  • Final count of all instruments and gauze
  • Specimens labelled and documented
  • Wound dressed; documentation completed
  • Assist with transfer to recovery
