Femoral Nail Insertion

Femoral Nail Insertion is a common orthopaedic procedure used to treat femoral shaft fractures. This guide outlines the anterior approach using the Stryker nailing system. As a scrub scout nurse, ensure thorough preparation of instruments, implants, and intraoperative steps for successful outcomes.

Relevant Anatomy

The procedure targets the femoral shaft and involves entry through the proximal femur. Knowledge of the anterior approach landmarks, including the greater trochanter and femoral canal alignment, is crucial.

1. Instruments and Equipment Checklist

Basic Ortho Tray (Sterile)

  • Scalpel blades (No. 10, 15)
  • Metzenbaum and Mayo scissors
  • Kocher and Allis forceps
  • Hohmann retractors (small, medium, large)
  • Yankauer suction tip
  • Needle holders
  • Tissue forceps (toothed and non-toothed)
  • Power drill (Stryker or similar)
  • Pulse lavage system

Stryker Femoral Nailing Set

  • Guide wire set
  • Entry reamers
  • Canal reamers (flexible/rigid)
  • Nail inserter and extractor
  • Targeting arm and alignment jig
  • Distal locking bolts and sleeves
  • Interlocking screw set
  • End caps and depth gauges

Sutures and Wound Closure

  • 2-0, 3-0 Vicryl for deep layers
  • 3-0 Monocryl or staples for skin
  • Steri-strips or wound glue (optional)

Other Equipment

  • C-arm (image intensifier)
  • Lead gowns and thyroid shields
  • Tourniquet (if required)
  • Warm irrigation fluids
  • Prep and drape packs (including leg drapes)
  • Antibiotic cement (if needed)

2. Before Knife to Skin

  1. Open and check all trays, including Stryker nail set and power tools
  2. Complete and document initial instrument count
  3. Position and pad patient appropriately
  4. Ensure image intensifier is working and accessible
  5. Confirm correct nail length and diameter availability

Patient Positioning

  • Position: Supine on a radiolucent traction table or flat table
  • Traction: Apply if using traction table, with foot boot and perineal post
  • C-arm: Ensure full-length femoral imaging possible
  • Padding: Pad pressure areas and secure patient safely

Skin Prepping and Draping

  • Prep entire leg from iliac crest to toes with chlorhexidine or iodine
  • Use sterile drapes to isolate operative limb, maintain access to C-arm
  • Apply limb isolation drape or clear drape for anterior approach exposure

3. Intraoperative Stages

  1. Incision: Anterior skin incision made proximal to greater trochanter
  2. Entry Point: Identify entry with image guidance; use awl and guide wire
  3. Canal Preparation: Sequential reaming to desired nail diameter
  4. Nail Insertion: Insert femoral nail over guide wire
  5. Locking Screws: Use targeting arm for proximal locking; free-hand or jig for distal screws
  6. Confirm Placement: Use C-arm to confirm nail and screw positioning
  7. Irrigation and Closure: Irrigate thoroughly; close in layers

4. Post-Op Tasks

  • Final instrument and swab count
  • Assist with drape removal and patient transfer
  • Clean and organise instruments for sterilisation
  • Document implants used (size, lot numbers)
  • Assist with PACU or ward transfer and handover
